# Legal Landscape Review: Digital Inheritance

## 1. Legal Status of Digital Assets
### Ownership vs. Licensing
- **Problem**: Many "assets" (SaaS accounts, Social Media) are licensed, not owned. Terms of Service (ToS) often forbid account transfer.
- **Mitigation**: Focus on the "Right of Access" and "Right to Data" rather than "Ownership." Use legal power of attorney templates to guide beneficiaries.

### Jurisdictional Variation
- **USA**: Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access Tools Act (RUFADAA) provides a framework for executors to access digital assets.
- **EU**: GDPR provides "Right to be Forgotten" but also allows for the exercise of rights by heirs in some jurisdictions.
- **Global**: High variability. The system must allow users to specify their jurisdiction and apply the relevant "Legal Workflow Layer."

## 2. The Role of the Fiduciary (Executor/Trustee)
### Legal Authority
- The system must support the upload of verified death certificates and legal probate documents.
- AI cannot be the legal executor; it must facilitate the human executor's duties.

### Liability
- Executors may face liability if they transfer assets incorrectly. The system's **Audit Trail** (Layer 7) is critical for protecting the executor.

## 3. Compliance Requirements
### KYC/AML
- When transferring high-value cryptocurrency or corporate entities, the system must ensure the beneficiary has completed necessary identity verification.

### Tax Implications
- Digital assets are taxable in most jurisdictions. The system should generate a "Transfer Log" for tax reporting.

## 4. Legal Workflow Design
1. **Asset Registry**: Map assets to legal categories (Owned, Licensed, Custodial).
2. **Instruction Set**: User-defined "Will" (Digital Testament).
3. **Verification**: Multi-party confirmation (Lawyer + Medical Proof + Trustee).
4. **Execution**: Progressive disclosure of secrets.
